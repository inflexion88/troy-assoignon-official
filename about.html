Field notes from the 20-day story

This is the hero’s journey version. I share it with clients so they know I’m not theorizing — I’ve lived the chaos they’re navigating.

  • Debt trenches + drywall dust

    I ran construction crews across Winnipeg, kept twenty families paid, and watched a $90K invoice vanish weeks before Christmas. That pain made me obsessed with positioning — because poor positioning is why we competed on price.

  • Night shift education

    I earned a bank security clearance and spent nights with one headphone, studying marketing frameworks while building million-dollar interiors. I sold equipment to invest $10K into a mentor when I couldn’t afford it. That was the leap into strategic work.

  • Community builder

    We launched an entrepreneur collective that reached 10,000+ people, secured sponsors like Audi and Bacardi, and gave founders a space to be honest. Listening to those rooms shaped the diagnostics I still use.

  • Alecia + Florida + a new operating system

    Alecia and I road-tripped through Florida, slept in Walmart lots, meditated at sunrise, and decided to build a business that fit the life we wanted. That commitment drove us to run advisory engagements from Mexico, Thailand, Vietnam, and across Canada.

  • First advisory transformations

    A service business went from -$300 to +$4K monthly cash flow. A construction client took contracts from $3K to $20K. A coach jumped from $800 to $50K retainers. Documenting those wins whenever they happened became my proof discipline.

  • Partnership scars + integrity

    In Bali, a partner vanished mid launch. I refunded, restructured, and finished the delivery solo. That experience cemented my rule: no strategy leaves the war room unless I can defend it personally.

  • Nomad reps & resilience

    We returned to find our Vancouver condo trashed, rebuilt, then moved to Thailand with two suitcases. I ran investor trainings from Chiang Mai rooftops while unwinding lost wire transfers. Chaos forced me to build the systems clients use today.

  • Near-death wake-up call

    I flipped my truck three times after a 24-hour workday. Hanging upside down with blood on my arms reminded me the operator is the asset. Now I hold myself and every founder I coach to a holistic standard: health + proof + positioning.
