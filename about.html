I grew up in construction, learned marketing at midnight with an N95 mask on, and built a 10K-person entrepreneur community before positioning became my obsession. Those reps taught me how to earn attention without gimmicks. Today I work with founders, investors, and executive teams who want more than another campaign — they want a position that defends premium pricing and commands investor confidence.

Why positioning is the lever I pull

Teams bring me in when budgets are on the line. They cannot explain why they belong in a prospect’s spreadsheet, sit on proof they never curate, or pitch investors with stories no one cares to fund. Positioning fixes that upstream. Define the territory. Design the proof. Defend the price. When those three steps snap into place, sales, marketing, and capital raises become easier to execute.

Field notes from a twenty day story

I recently documented the long-form journey from job sites to strategic rooms. These chapters shape how I operate with every client.

  • Construction collapse and debt trenches

    I spent years running crews across Winnipeg, chasing six figure invoices that never landed, and keeping twenty families paid even when I was suffocating under debt. That season taught me accountability and the cost of weak positioning the hard way.

  • One headphone, one mentor, a $10K bet

    I earned a bank security clearance, wore a single headphone on night shifts, and packed it with marketing courses. Selling equipment to join a mentor’s program became the inflection point that moved me from job sites to strategy rooms.

  • Building a 10K-person community

    We launched events that drew hundreds each month, partnered with Audi and Bacardi, and created a space where founders could be honest. Power washing drywall dust off in a Subway bathroom before those sessions kept me humble and listener-first.

  • Meeting Alecia and rewriting the script

    We camped through Florida, slept in Walmart parking lots, meditated beside the ocean, and decided to build a life around work we believed in. She challenged me to heal, travel, and push my craft far beyond “get by” mode.

  • Early advisory wins

    One service business swung from negative three hundred dollars a month to plus four thousand. A construction client jumped from $3K contracts to $20K. A coach went from $800 retainers to $50K engagements. Documenting those wins became my proof discipline.

  • Partnership scars

    In Bali I took over a program after a partner disappeared mid launch. I refunded, restructured, and delivered solo. Ending a five-year friendship hurt, but it forced me to vet collaborators and protect client outcomes at all costs.

  • Nomad reps & resilience

    We came home to a trashed apartment, rebuilt, then left for Thailand with two suitcases. I ran investor trainings in Vietnam, navigated lost wire transfers, and kept clients happy from Chiang Mai rooftops. Chaos taught me to stay calm and operational.

  • Near-death wake up call

    I flipped my truck three times after a twenty-four hour workday. Hanging upside down with blood on my arms reminded me that execution requires rest, systems, and personal integrity. I hold myself and my clients to that same standard now.

How I work with clients now

I collaborate with founders, investors, and executive teams who need a clear position to defend premium pricing or to tell a story worth funding. Every engagement runs through the Strategic Positioning Advantage™:

  1. Define. Map incentives, competitors, market myths, and valuation triggers so we know exactly which territory is worth owning.
  2. Design. Architect offers, proof libraries, pricing ladders, internal talk tracks, and media scripts that reinforce the position everywhere.
  3. Defend. Operationalize the playbooks so sales, marketing, and leadership protect the new territory under pressure.

Life now

Alecia and I split time between Playa del Carmen and client onsite sessions. When I am not in a war room, I am training Brazilian jiu-jitsu, writing long-form essays, and coaching founders through the same holistic lens that got me out of construction. I still document every meaningful result because proof keeps you grounded and confident in every room.
