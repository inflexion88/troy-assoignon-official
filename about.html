Why positioning is the lever I pull

Teams hire me when they cannot explain why they belong in a budget line. They sit on proof they never curate, discount to stay alive, or pitch investors without a story worth funding. Positioning is the upstream fix. Define the territory. Design the proof. Defend the price. Everything else gets easier when that is nailed.

Field notes from a twenty day story

I recently told the long version of how I arrived here over a twenty day thread. These chapters shaped how I operate today.

  • Construction collapse and debt trenches

    I spent years running crews across Winnipeg, chasing six figure invoices that never landed, and keeping twenty families paid even when I was suffocating under debt. That season taught me accountability and the cost of weak positioning the hard way.

  • Headphones, N95 masks, and a $10K bet

    I earned a bank security clearance, wore a single headphone on night shifts, and packed it with marketing courses. I sold gear to join a mentor‚Äôs program even though I was broke. That mix of self taught design chops and relentless study became the foundation of my consulting work.

  • Building a 10K person community

    We launched an event series that drew hundreds each month, partnered with Audi and Bacardi, and gave founders a place to be honest. I would power wash drywall dust off in a Subway bathroom before hosting those nights. That grind showed me how many operators lacked strategic counsel.

  • Meeting Alecia and choosing a different life

    Alecia and I first connected online, then spent three weeks camping across Florida. We slept in Walmart parking lots, meditated beside the ocean, and talked about building something that matched the way we wanted to live. She challenged me to heal and to take my skills abroad.

  • Early advisory wins

    I helped a service business swing from negative three hundred dollars a month to plus four thousand. A construction client took contracts from three grand to twenty grand. A coach jumped from eight hundred to fifty thousand per engagement. Documenting those wins became non negotiable.

  • Partnership scars

    In Bali I took over a program after a partner vanished mid launch while playing video games. I refunded, restructured, and finished the delivery solo. It hurt to end a five year friendship, yet it forced me to hold collaborators to a higher bar.

  • Nomad reps and resilience

    We returned to Vancouver to find a tenant had trashed our apartment, rebuilt, and then left for Thailand with everything we owned stuffed into two suitcases. I trained teams in Vietnam, handled wire transfers that went missing, and kept clients happy from Chiang Mai rooftops.

  • Near-death wake up call

    Before all of this I flipped my truck three times at five in the morning after a twenty four hour workday. Hanging upside down with blood on my arms reminded me that performance requires rest. I hold myself and my clients to that same standard now.

How I work with clients now

I partner with founders, investors, and executive teams who need to command premium pricing or ship investor grade stories. Engagements follow one cadence:

  1. Define. Map incentives, competitors, customer beliefs, and valuation triggers.
  2. Design. Architect proof libraries, pricing ladders, talk tracks, and media scripts.
  3. Defend. Operationalize the new position through training, assets, and internal adoption.

I still log every meaningful result because proof is what keeps you grounded.
