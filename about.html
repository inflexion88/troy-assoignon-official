The Strategic Positioning Advantage™ Framework

Unlike generic brand consultants who start with logos and messaging, I use a systematic three-phase methodology:

Phase 1: Define

Establish your strategic foundation through competitive landscape mapping, value attribute analysis, and category ownership opportunity identification. No guesswork — we diagnose exactly where you should own market territory.

Phase 2: Design

Architect the narrative system that communicates your position across every stakeholder touchpoint. Core story development, proof architecture, and message hierarchy that adapts from 10-second pitches to 10-minute presentations.

Phase 3: Defend

Build competitive insulation through moat identification, premium pricing architecture, and thought leadership positioning. This is where positioning becomes permanent competitive advantage, not temporary marketing campaign.

Why My Approach Is Different

Most positioning consultants come from advertising or brand agencies. They treat positioning as creative work — subjective, iterative, opinion-based.

I approach positioning differently, informed by 30+ years in construction project management before transitioning to strategic consulting. In construction, you don't start building without blueprints. You don't guess at foundation depth. You follow systematic processes based on site conditions, building codes, and structural engineering principles.

Positioning works the same way. You can't design messaging before you've mapped the competitive landscape. You can't build market position without understanding the structural moats that will defend it. You need blueprints, not brainstorms.

The Construction-Positioning Connection

My construction background isn't just context — it's the foundation of my methodology:

  • • Foundation before walls: Positioning before marketing (most companies do this backwards)
  • • Blueprint methodology: Systematic process, not creative guesswork
  • • Load-bearing walls: Strategic elements that hold competitive advantage vs. decorative elements (features, messaging)
  • • Building codes: Proven standards that prevent positioning collapse (defensibility, proof requirements, consistency)

This systematic approach is why my clients see measurable results, not just better-looking brand decks.
