Positioning Audit Checklist: 23-Point Self-Assessment

Quick Assessment Overview

This 23-point audit measures positioning strength across four dimensions:

  1. Strategic Clarity (6 questions): Do you have clear, documented positioning?
  2. Competitive Differentiation (6 questions): Are you meaningfully different from competitors?
  3. Proof & Evidence (5 questions): Can you substantiate your positioning claims?
  4. Market Impact (6 questions): Is your positioning delivering business results?

Scoring: Answer each question with 0 (No), 1 (Somewhat), 2 (Mostly), or 3 (Definitely Yes). Total score out of 69 points.

Interpretation:

  • 55-69: Strong positioning (minor refinements)
  • 35-54: Moderate positioning (strategic updates needed)
  • 0-34: Weak positioning (comprehensive repositioning required)

Most companies operate with weak or undifferentiated positioning—and don't realize it until they're trapped in price-based competition with eroding margins.

This 23-point audit helps you diagnose your positioning strength before it becomes a revenue problem. Use it to:

  • Identify strategic gaps in your positioning
  • Benchmark against positioning best practices
  • Prioritize repositioning initiatives by impact
  • Determine if you need professional positioning diagnostics

Time required: 20-30 minutes for honest self-assessment

How to Use This Audit

Scoring System

For each question, assign a score:

  • 0 = No / Not at all – This doesn't describe us
  • 1 = Somewhat – We partially meet this criterion
  • 2 = Mostly – We largely meet this, with minor gaps
  • 3 = Definitely Yes – We fully meet this criterion

Be brutally honest. Grade yourself as a tough buyer would, not how you want to be perceived.

Section 1: Strategic Clarity (18 points possible)

Strong positioning starts with clarity: you must know exactly who you serve, what you stand for, and how you're different.

Question 1: Can you clearly define your target audience?

What to assess: Do you have a specific, narrow target audience beyond "all businesses" or "mid-market companies"?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Highly specific targeting (e.g., "Series A-C growth-stage B2B SaaS companies in financial services")
  • 2 points: Somewhat specific (e.g., "B2B SaaS companies in growth stage")
  • 1 point: Broad but not meaningless (e.g., "B2B companies")
  • 0 points: No targeting (e.g., "We serve all businesses")

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 2: Can you articulate your value proposition in one sentence?

What to assess: Can you clearly state what value you deliver and to whom, in a single sentence?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: One clear sentence specifying who you serve, what problem you solve, and how you're uniquely positioned
  • 2 points: One sentence that captures what you do, but lacks specificity
  • 1 point: A sentence exists, but it's jargon-heavy or unclear
  • 0 points: No clear value proposition

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 3: Do you own a specific category or position in the market?

What to assess: When buyers think of your category, do they think of you?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: You own a defined category where you're the clear leader
  • 2 points: You have a category you compete in, recognized as a top player
  • 1 point: You're in a category but not differentiated within it
  • 0 points: You compete in generic categories with no differentiation

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 4: Do you have a documented positioning statement?

What to assess: Is your positioning formally documented with all four components: target audience, competitive frame, unique differentiation, and value delivered?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Formal positioning statement documented and shared across leadership, sales, and marketing
  • 2 points: Positioning is documented but missing one component or not widely shared
  • 1 point: Informal positioning exists but not documented
  • 0 points: No formal positioning statement

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 5: Does your entire team understand and align on your positioning?

What to assess: If you asked 5 employees "What makes us different?" would they give consistent answers?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: 100% of sales/marketing/leadership can articulate positioning consistently
  • 2 points: Most team members (70%+) can articulate positioning, though wording varies
  • 1 point: Some team members (30-70%) understand positioning, but answers are inconsistent
  • 0 points: Team members give wildly different answers

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 6: Can your sales team explain why a customer should choose you (without mentioning price)?

What to assess: Does your sales team lead with strategic differentiation, or default to features/discounting?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Sales consistently leads with strategic positioning and value differentiation
  • 2 points: Sales can articulate differentiation but often falls back to features
  • 1 point: Sales mentions differentiation but relies heavily on price/terms to close
  • 0 points: Sales competes primarily on price or relationship

Your score (0-3): _____

Section 1 Total (Strategic Clarity): _____ / 18

Section 2: Competitive Differentiation (18 points possible)

Positioning is fundamentally competitive—it's about owning territory that competitors don't own.

Question 7: Do you deliver value that competitors can't easily replicate?

What to assess: Is your differentiation based on proprietary capabilities or unique assets?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Differentiation based on proprietary methodologies, deep specialization, or unique assets that take years to build
  • 2 points: You have differentiation that's somewhat difficult to copy
  • 1 point: Your differentiation is easily replicable
  • 0 points: No meaningful differentiation

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 8: When buyers compare you to competitors, is the difference clear?

What to assess: Do buyers see you as meaningfully different, or lump you in with alternatives?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Buyers consistently say "you're the only one who does X"
  • 2 points: Buyers acknowledge differences, but still compare you to 2-3 alternatives
  • 1 point: Buyers see minor differences but consider you "basically similar"
  • 0 points: Buyers say "you all seem the same"

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 9: Have you created meaningful switching costs or lock-in?

What to assess: Once a customer works with you, is it difficult to switch to a competitor?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: High switching costs due to proprietary processes or integration depth
  • 2 points: Moderate switching costs; inconvenient but not costly
  • 1 point: Low switching costs; customers could replace you relatively easily
  • 0 points: No switching costs; you're easily replaced

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 10: Do you track win/loss analysis to understand competitive dynamics?

What to assess: Do you systematically analyze why you win and lose deals?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Formal win/loss analysis for all significant deals with documented patterns
  • 2 points: Informal win/loss tracking; generally know why but not documented
  • 1 point: Occasional win/loss analysis, not consistent
  • 0 points: No win/loss analysis

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 11: Do you own "whitespace" territory that competitors haven't claimed?

What to assess: Have you identified and claimed valuable market position that competitors aren't targeting?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: You've identified and claimed strategically valuable but unclaimed whitespace
  • 2 points: You compete in a position with some differentiation but others are moving into it
  • 1 point: Your position has minor whitespace elements but largely overlaps with competitors
  • 0 points: You compete in saturated territory with no whitespace

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 12: Do you have a "moat"—a defensible competitive advantage?

What to assess: What prevents competitors from displacing you?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Multiple moats that compound (proprietary methodology + specialized expertise + category leadership)
  • 2 points: One clear moat that provides meaningful protection
  • 1 point: Weak moats that provide minimal protection
  • 0 points: No moats; competitors can replicate everything you do

Your score (0-3): _____

Section 2 Total (Competitive Differentiation): _____ / 18

Section 3: Proof & Evidence (15 points possible)

Positioning claims without proof = buyer skepticism.

Question 13: Do you have case studies with documented, quantified results?

What to assess: Can you show buyers concrete proof that your positioning delivers results?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: 5+ documented case studies with specific metrics and client attribution
  • 2 points: 2-4 case studies with some metrics but incomplete documentation
  • 1 point: Generic "success stories" without specific metrics
  • 0 points: No case studies or documented results

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 14: Can you quantify the ROI of working with you?

What to assess: Do you know—and can you articulate—the financial return clients get?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Documented ROI across multiple clients with specific timeframes
  • 2 points: ROI data for some clients, not systematically tracked
  • 1 point: General sense of ROI but no specific documentation
  • 0 points: Cannot quantify ROI

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 15: Do you have client testimonials that speak to your positioning?

What to assess: Do testimonials reinforce your strategic position, or are they generic praise?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Testimonials specifically reference your positioning
  • 2 points: Testimonials mention outcomes but don't tie directly to positioning
  • 1 point: Generic testimonials ("professional," "responsive")
  • 0 points: No testimonials or only anonymous quotes

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 16: Do you demonstrate specialization through content or thought leadership?

What to assess: Have you published frameworks or insights that demonstrate positioning expertise?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Published frameworks/methodologies, regular thought leadership, recognized as category expert
  • 2 points: Some content published, occasional thought leadership
  • 1 point: Generic blog posts or marketing content, no unique frameworks
  • 0 points: No thought leadership or published expertise

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 17: Do you have third-party validation or credibility indicators?

What to assess: Beyond your own claims, do external sources validate your positioning?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Multiple third-party validations: media mentions, industry awards, analyst recognition
  • 2 points: Some third-party validation (client logos, occasional media mentions)
  • 1 point: Minimal third-party validation
  • 0 points: No external validation

Your score (0-3): _____

Section 3 Total (Proof & Evidence): _____ / 15

Section 4: Market Impact (18 points possible)

The ultimate test: is your positioning delivering business results?

Question 18: Can you command premium pricing vs. generalist competitors?

What to assess: Do you charge 15-30%+ more than less-differentiated alternatives?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: You consistently price 25%+ above generalist competitors and win deals
  • 2 points: You price 10-25% above competitors and occasionally win at premium
  • 1 point: Your pricing is roughly equivalent to competitors
  • 0 points: You discount to win deals or compete primarily on price

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 19: Are your profit margins expanding (or maintaining premium levels)?

What to assess: Do you maintain 30%+ margins?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Gross margins of 35%+ and growing
  • 2 points: Margins of 25-35%, stable but not expanding
  • 1 point: Margins of 15-25%, under pressure
  • 0 points: Margins below 15% or declining year-over-year

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 20: Is your sales cycle efficient?

What to assess: Does clear positioning accelerate buyer decisions?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Sales cycles are 30-50% shorter than industry average
  • 2 points: Sales cycles are average for your industry
  • 1 point: Sales cycles are longer than average due to competitive evaluation
  • 0 points: Extended sales cycles (6+ months) with heavy negotiation

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 21: Do you attract high-quality inbound inquiries?

What to assess: Are inbound leads pre-qualified by your positioning?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Inbound leads are pre-qualified, understand your positioning, higher close rates
  • 2 points: Inbound leads are somewhat qualified but require education
  • 1 point: Inbound leads are mostly price shoppers
  • 0 points: No meaningful inbound; you rely entirely on outbound

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 22: Are you perceived as the category leader in your positioning niche?

What to assess: When people think of your category, do they think of you first?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: You're the default choice in your category
  • 2 points: You're one of the top 3 names mentioned
  • 1 point: You're known in your category but not top-of-mind
  • 0 points: Low category awareness

Your score (0-3): _____

Question 23: Is revenue growing because of positioning strength?

What to assess: Can you attribute revenue growth to stronger positioning?

Scoring guide:

  • 3 points: Revenue growth is directly attributable to positioning improvements
  • 2 points: Revenue is growing, and positioning contributes but isn't the primary driver
  • 1 point: Revenue growth is flat or only due to market expansion
  • 0 points: Revenue is declining or stagnant despite market growth

Your score (0-3): _____

Score Interpretation & Next Steps

55-69 Points: Strong Positioning (Minor Refinements)

What it means: Your positioning is fundamentally sound. Focus on optimization, not overhaul.

Recommended actions:

  • Identify 2-3 lowest-scoring questions and address those specific gaps
  • Strengthen proof points (case studies, ROI documentation, testimonials)
  • Expand thought leadership to reinforce category ownership
  • Consider positioning diagnostics to find optimization opportunities

Timeline: 30-60 days to address refinements

35-54 Points: Moderate Positioning (Strategic Updates Needed)

What it means: You have positioning foundations but significant gaps. You're vulnerable to competitive displacement or commoditization.

Recommended actions:

  • Conduct formal positioning diagnostics to identify highest-impact improvements
  • Narrow your target audience to enable category ownership
  • Develop documented case studies with quantified results
  • Update positioning statement and align team on differentiation
  • Consider strategic repositioning engagement (60-90 days)

Timeline: 90-120 days for strategic repositioning

0-34 Points: Weak Positioning (Comprehensive Repositioning Required)

What it means: Your positioning is fundamentally weak. You're likely competing on price, struggling with commoditization, and experiencing margin pressure. This is a critical business issue.

Recommended actions:

  • Immediate: Engage comprehensive positioning strategy to define competitive territory and value architecture
  • Conduct competitive research to identify whitespace opportunities
  • Narrow target audience dramatically (3-5x narrower than current)
  • Document value delivered with case studies and ROI metrics
  • Implement positioning across website, sales, and marketing

Cost of inaction: Weak positioning leads to 15-25% pricing discounts, 50-70% lower margins vs. specialists, and competitive displacement.

Timeline: 90-120 days for comprehensive repositioning + 60 days for implementation

Next Steps Based on Your Score

If you scored 55+:

You're in good shape. Focus on:

  • Strengthening proof points and thought leadership
  • Expanding into adjacent positioning territory
  • Building competitive moats to maintain your lead

If you scored 35-54:

You need strategic updates. Consider:

  • Positioning diagnostics to identify highest-impact improvements
  • Narrowing your target audience for category ownership
  • Developing documented case studies and ROI proof

If you scored 0-34:

You need comprehensive repositioning. Start with:

  • Full positioning strategy engagement to define competitive territory
  • Competitive research to find defensible whitespace
  • Value architecture design to enable premium pricing

Need Help Interpreting Your Score?

If you scored below 55, you likely have positioning gaps that are costing you pricing power and margin expansion. A professional positioning diagnostic identifies specific improvements and prioritizes them by revenue impact.
