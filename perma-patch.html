Positioning Canvas

1. Competitive Alternatives

What are customers using today if not your product?

  • Traditional cold patch products. Harden in winter storage, require dry weather
  • Hot mix asphalt. Requires equipment, large crews, dry weather windows
  • Temporary pothole fixes. Require repeated callbacks, waste budget
  • Commodity repair products. Not portable, harden in warehouses during winter
  • Original Permapatch. Doesn't harden as much as customers expect

2. Unique Capabilities

What can you do that alternatives cannot?

  • Water-activated curing. Cures and hardens instantly with water
  • All-weather performance. Rain, snow, shine, conditions don't matter
  • Consistent consistency. Same every time, doesn't harden in storage
  • Thin application capability. Spread thinner than competing products
  • Multi-application versatility. Works in feathering, potholes, and cutouts
  • Eco-conscious formula. Water-reactive binder free of solvents and VOCs
  • Effortless deployment. Pours easily from pail, no wait, no prep, no mess
  • Superior bonding. Lasts longer than the pavement around it

  • "Effortless application". Just wet it, set it and forget it
  • "No wait, no prep, no mess"

Strategic Benefits (Deeper Fears Addressed):

Ultimate Value: Regaining control over pavement degradation. Giving municipalities back control over infrastructure that feels out of control.

Implementation Checklist

This positioning only works if it shapes every decision you make. Use this checklist to activate your new strategic position across all customer touchpoints.

Phase 1: Internal Alignment (Week 1-2)

  • Sales Team Training: Shift all conversations from feature language (water-activated, fast-setting) to value language (all-conditions control, regaining power over infrastructure)
  • Messaging Audit: Review all existing materials (website, decks, brochures) and identify everywhere you're using commodity "pothole repair" language
  • Team Workshop: Host session where everyone practices the new positioning statement and can articulate why you're "not just better, you're different"
  • Competitive Intel: Document exactly how competitors fail in conditions where you thrive (create comparison matrix)

Phase 2: Proof Points & Evidence (Week 2-4)

  • Emergency Repair Case Studies: Gather testimonials from municipalities who used Permapatch Aqua in crisis situations or extreme weather
  • Before/After Stories: Document departments that regained control over their repair backlog using your solution
  • Performance Data: Compile quantitative evidence of performance in extreme weather vs. competitors
  • Cost Analysis: Build ROI calculator showing one-time repair vs. repeated callbacks with traditional products
  • Crew Efficiency Metrics: Document time savings and crew size reductions from actual customers

Phase 3: Demo & Sales Process (Week 3-5)

  • Demo Strategy Shift: Stop demonstrating in perfect conditions. Always demo in challenging weather (rain, cold, wet) where competitors fail
  • "Works When Nothing Else Does" Demo: Create signature demo showing application in rain or cold with minimal crew
  • Sales Deck Overhaul: Lead with the problem (loss of control), introduce the category (all-conditions control), then position Permapatch Aqua as the category creator
  • Discovery Questions: Train team to ask about control, crew morale, looking incompetent, not just about product features they want
  • Competitor Positioning: Frame alternatives as "conditional repair" vs. your "unconditional control"

Phase 4: Marketing & Content (Week 4-8)

  • Website Redesign: Homepage hero should communicate category position, not product features
  • Content Strategy: Create educational content about "all-conditions infrastructure control" to own the category
  • Email Campaigns: Segment by pain point (winter repair challenges, crew efficiency issues, budget pressures)
  • Video Content: Capture real crews using product in challenging conditions with testimonials about regaining control
  • PR Strategy: Position founder/team as thought leaders on next-gen infrastructure repair and climate adaptation

Phase 5: Go-to-Market Execution (Ongoing)

  • Trade Show Presence: Booth messaging should focus on category creation, not product comparison

Key Questions to Keep You Honest

Before any customer interaction, ask yourself:

  • Am I framing this around "all-conditions control" or falling back into feature talk?
  • Am I demonstrating in conditions where competitors fail, or playing it safe?
  • Does my proof point reinforce "works when nothing else does"?
  • Am I positioning as category creator or just better product?
