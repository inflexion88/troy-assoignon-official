Positioning Clarity Assessment Questions

How clearly can you articulate what makes your offering unique in the market?

  • Very clearly - I can explain it in one sentence
  • Pretty clearly - I can explain it in a short paragraph
  • Somewhat clearly - It takes a few minutes to explain
  • Not very clearly - I struggle to explain our uniqueness
  • Not at all - We haven't defined our unique position

Do all team members describe your company's position in the market consistently?

  • Yes - Everyone uses the same positioning language
  • Mostly - There's general alignment with minor variations
  • Somewhat - Some consistency but noticeable differences
  • Not really - Different people say different things
  • Not at all - There's no consistent positioning message

How well documented is your positioning strategy?

  • Fully documented - Complete positioning framework with all elements
  • Well documented - Key elements captured in writing
  • Partially documented - Some notes and informal guidelines
  • Minimally documented - Mostly exists in people's heads
  • Not documented - No written positioning strategy exists

Can you clearly state which market category you compete in?

  • Yes - We own or define a specific category
  • Yes - We have a clear category with slight nuances
  • Somewhat - We fit into multiple categories
  • Not really - Our category is unclear or too broad
  • No - We haven't defined our market category

Differentiation Strength Assessment Questions

What makes your offering meaningfully different from competitors?

  • Unique innovation - We offer something no one else does
  • Strong differentiation - We have clear, defendable differences
  • Moderate differentiation - Some differences but not game-changing
  • Weak differentiation - Minor differences, mostly similar to competitors
  • No differentiation - We compete primarily on price

How defensible is your competitive advantage?

  • Highly defensible - Significant barriers (IP, network effects, etc.)
  • Defensible - Takes significant time/resources to replicate
  • Moderately defensible - Could be copied but with effort
  • Weakly defensible - Easy for competitors to replicate
  • Not defensible - No moat or competitive barriers

How often do prospects say "you're just like [competitor]"?

  • Never - We're always seen as unique
  • Rarely - Occasionally compared but clearly different
  • Sometimes - We get compared to competitors regularly
  • Often - Frequently lumped in with competitors
  • Always - Seen as commoditized/interchangeable

Do you win deals based on unique value or primarily on price?

  • Always on value - Price is rarely the deciding factor
  • Mostly on value - Price matters but value wins
  • Mixed - Equal split between value and price wins
  • Mostly on price - We often need to discount to win
  • Always on price - We compete primarily as low-cost option

Target Market Alignment Assessment Questions

How well-defined is your ideal customer profile (ICP)?

  • Crystal clear - Detailed ICP with specific firmographics/demographics
  • Well-defined - Clear ICP with key characteristics
  • Somewhat defined - General sense of our target market
  • Poorly defined - Broad or vague target market
  • Not defined - We try to serve everyone

What percentage of your sales leads match your ideal customer profile?

  • 80-100% - Nearly all leads are in our sweet spot
  • 60-79% - Most leads are a good fit
  • 40-59% - About half are a good fit
  • 20-39% - Many leads are poor fits
  • 0-19% - Most leads are not our ideal customers

Do you say "no" to customers who don't fit your ideal profile?

  • Always - We're disciplined about our ICP
  • Usually - We turn away poor fits most of the time
  • Sometimes - We're selective but flexible
  • Rarely - We take most customers who can pay
  • Never - We serve anyone willing to buy

How well does your messaging resonate with your target audience?

  • Extremely well - Prospects say "this is exactly what I need"
  • Very well - Strong resonance and engagement
  • Moderately well - Some resonance but could be stronger
  • Not well - Generic reactions, low engagement
  • Poorly - Messaging doesn't land with our audience

Value Proposition Clarity Assessment Questions

Can you articulate your value proposition in one clear sentence?

  • Yes - Crystal clear, compelling one-sentence value prop
  • Yes - Clear value prop, maybe needs minor refinement
  • Somewhat - We have a value prop but it's a bit wordy
  • Not really - Our value prop is unclear or too complex
  • No - We don't have a defined value proposition

How quantifiable is the value you deliver to customers?

  • Highly quantifiable - We have specific ROI/metrics to prove value
  • Mostly quantifiable - We can demonstrate value with data
  • Partially quantifiable - Some metrics, mostly qualitative benefits
  • Barely quantifiable - Primarily qualitative/intangible value
  • Not quantifiable - We can't measure our value

Do customers understand the value before they buy?

  • Always - They "get it" immediately
  • Usually - Most understand value quickly
  • Sometimes - Takes some explanation
  • Rarely - Value only clear after they use product
  • Never - We struggle to communicate value upfront

Is your value proposition focused on outcomes or features?

  • Entirely outcomes - We sell business results and transformations
  • Mostly outcomes - Focus on results with some feature mentions
  • Mixed - Equal focus on outcomes and features
  • Mostly features - We lead with what we do, not results
  • Only features - We list capabilities without outcome focus

Competitive Position Assessment Questions

How well do you understand your competitive landscape?

  • Extremely well - Deep competitive analysis, ongoing monitoring
  • Very well - Strong understanding of key competitors
  • Moderately well - Basic competitive awareness
  • Not well - Limited competitive knowledge
  • Poorly - We don't track competitors

Where do you rank in your target market segment?

  • Market leader - #1 or #2 in our category
  • Strong player - Top 5 in our category
  • Mid-tier player - Recognized but not leading
  • Small player - Limited market presence
  • Unknown - No meaningful market position

How often do you appear in competitive evaluations?

  • Always - We're always in consideration sets
  • Usually - We're included in most evaluations
  • Sometimes - We're sometimes considered
  • Rarely - We're rarely in competitive deals
  • Never - Prospects don't know we exist

What's your win rate in competitive deals?

  • 70%+ - We win most competitive deals
  • 50-69% - We win more than we lose
  • 30-49% - We win less than we lose
  • 10-29% - We rarely win competitive deals
  • 0-9% - We almost never win when competing
