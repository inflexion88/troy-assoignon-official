Strategic Positioning Diagnostic

25-Point Positioning Diagnostic Checklist

The complete diagnostic framework used to generate $62.7M in documented client results. Evaluate your positioning across the three pillars: Define, Design, and Defend.

Pillar 1: Define Your Position

Category clarity, competitive differentiation, and market understanding

  • 1. Can you articulate your category position in 10 seconds?
    You should be able to explain what category you compete in and how you're different without jargon or vague claims. "We're an X that does Y" is not positioningâ€”it's a feature list.
  • 2. Do prospects immediately understand why you're different?
    If prospects need multiple meetings or a demo to understand your differentiation, your positioning is unclear. Strong positioning creates instant clarity.
  • 3. Have you identified pattern saturation in your category?
    Most categories have predictable patterns. Map your competitors' positioning to find white space where you can differentiate meaningfully.
  • 4. Do you own a specific problem that buyers recognize?
    Strong positioning starts with a problem that your audience already feels. If you're educating them on the problem first, you're fighting uphill.
  • 5. Can you name 3-5 direct competitors and explain exactly how you're different?
    If you say "we have no competitors," your positioning is too vague. If you say "everyone is a competitor," you haven't defined your position.
  • 6. Does your positioning reflect insight about your market?
    The best positioning communicates "we understand something about this market that others don't." Insight creates authority and trust.
  • 7. Are you positioned by value attributes that matter to buyers?
    Your differentiation must align with what buyers actually care about. Research what drives purchase decisions in your category, then position accordingly.
  • 8. Is your positioning defensible?
    Strong positioning is hard to copy. It's built on unique capabilities, specialized expertise, or proprietary insightâ€”not just marketing claims.

Pillar 2: Design Your Narrative

Story architecture, proof integration, and message consistency

  • 9. Do you have a structured narrative arc (Problem â†’ Insight â†’ Solution)?
    Every positioning narrative needs clear structure. Start with a problem they feel, reveal insight they haven't considered, present your solution as inevitable.
  • 10. Is your positioning consistent across all channels?
    Website, sales deck, email, social mediaâ€”every channel should communicate the same positioning. Inconsistency destroys credibility.
  • 11. Have you adapted your narrative for different time horizons?
    You need a 10-second version, 30-second version, 2-minute version, and 10-minute version. Each should build naturally on the previous one.
  • 12. Does your positioning include specific proof?
    Replace vague claims with verified results. "$4.5M raised" beats "helped raise capital." Specificity builds trust.
  • 13. Do you balance emotional appeal with logical proof?
    People buy emotionally and justify logically. Your positioning needs both: urgency + evidence, vision + validation, story + stats.
  • 14. Is your positioning adapted for different buyer psychographics?
    Different buyers need different emphasis. Some want innovation, others want safety. Adapt your narrative while maintaining core positioning.
  • 15. Do you have testimonials that reinforce your positioning?
    Generic testimonials ("great to work with!") don't help. You need testimonials that specifically validate your differentiation and value proposition.
  • 16. Is your positioning immediately visible in every customer touchpoint?
    From your website hero to your email signature, your positioning should be reinforced constantly. Repetition builds recognition.
  • 17. Do you have a documented positioning playbook?
    Your team needs written guidelines on how to communicate positioning across channels. Without documentation, messaging drifts.

Pillar 3: Defend Your Position

Market entrenchment, thought leadership, and competitive insulation

  • 18. Are you building thought leadership around your positioning?
    Publish content that demonstrates your unique insight. Thought leadership makes your positioning harder to copy and builds category authority.
  • 19. Do you have proprietary frameworks or methodologies?
    Named frameworks (like Strategic Positioning Advantageâ„¢) create intellectual property around your positioning. They're defensible and memorable.
  • 20. Are you consistently visible in your category?
    Positioning requires sustained visibility. You should be publishing, speaking, or contributing to category conversations regularly.
  • 21. Do you have partnership or authority signals that reinforce your position?
    Strategic partnerships, certifications, awards, or recognized clients all strengthen your positioning and make it harder to dismiss.
  • 22. Is your pricing aligned with your positioning?
    Premium positioning requires premium pricing. If your prices don't match your positioning, you're undermining your own credibility.
  • 23. Do you track positioning effectiveness over time?
    Measure brand recall, message clarity in sales conversations, and whether prospects understand your differentiation. Positioning should improve conversion rates.
  • 24. Are you building barriers to competitive replication?
    Network effects, specialized expertise, proprietary data, or unique partnerships create moats. Your positioning should be rooted in something competitors can't easily copy.
  • 25. Do you have a plan to maintain positioning as you scale?
    As companies grow, positioning often dilutes. You need processes to maintain message consistency, onboard new team members on positioning, and resist category drift.

Real-World Application: Real Estate Investment Fund

The Challenge: A real estate investment fund had been pitching investors without success despite a strong team and proven track record. Their positioning was too broad: "commercial real estate investment."

The Diagnostic: Using this checklist, we identified critical gaps:

  • Pattern saturation: 30+ funds with identical broad positioning
  • Missing insight: No clear differentiation on what mattered to investors
  • Weak proof integration: Generic claims instead of specific evidence

The Solution: We repositioned around a specific property type within a specific geography, emphasizing deal flow exclusivity and downside protection over historical returns.
